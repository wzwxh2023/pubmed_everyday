## 1. 结节性硬化症相关癫痫小鼠模型中睡眠与癫痫发作的双向相互作用及食欲素研究

**原文链接：** [点击跳转](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41677467)
**期刊：** Epilepsia
**PMID：** 41677467
**DOI：** 10.1002/epi.70151

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** Bidirectional sleep-seizure interactions and orexin in a mouse model of tuberous sclerosis complex-related epilepsy.

> **英文摘要：**
> OBJECTIVE: A strong bidirectional relationship exists between epilepsy and sleep, with seizures often occurring more frequently in sleep and, in turn, sleep being disrupted by seizures. However, the mechanistic basis of seizure-sleep interactions is poorly understood. In this study, we characterized the relationship between seizures and sleep in a mouse model of the genetic disorder tuberous sclerosis complex (TSC) and investigated potential mechanisms underlying seizure-sleep interactions related to hypothalamic orexin.
> 
> METHODS: Tsc1CKO mice were used to investigate the relationship between seizures and sleep with video, electroencephalographic (EEG), and electromyographic analysis, including the dependence of seizure frequency on vigilance state (awake, rapid eye movement [REM], non-REM) and the effect of seizures on the wake-sleep cycle. Orexin expression in hypothalamus was assessed by immunohistochemistry in relation to seizure frequency. The effect of orexin antagonists on sleep and seizures was tested by video-EEG.
> 
> RESULTS: Overall seizures occurred most commonly in non-REM sleep in Tsc1CKO mice but had the highest seizure frequency in REM sleep after normalizing for amount of time spent in each vigilance state. Conversely, seizures were associated with disruptions in the sleep-wake cycle, particularly a decrease in REM sleep. Tsc1CKO mice with seizures had increased orexin expression compared with control mice or Tsc1CKO mice without seizures. The orexin antagonist suvorexant reversed the decrease in REM sleep but had no significant effect on seizures in Tsc1CKO mice.
> 
> SIGNIFICANCE: A bidirectional relationship between seizures and sleep was demonstrated in a mouse model of TSC, with relative seizure frequency surprisingly being highest in REM sleep and seizures causing a disruption of REM sleep. Hypothalamic orexin may partly mediate these seizure-sleep interactions, and orexin antagonists may represent rational therapies for sleep disorders in TSC.

> **中文摘要：**
> 目的：癫痫与睡眠之间存在强烈的双向关系，癫痫发作通常在睡眠中更频繁发生，反之，睡眠也会受到癫痫发作的干扰。然而，癫痫发作与睡眠相互作用的机制基础尚不明确。在本研究中，我们描述了遗传性疾病结节性硬化症（TSC）小鼠模型中癫痫发作与睡眠之间的关系，并探讨了与下丘脑食欲素（orexin）相关的癫痫-睡眠相互作用的潜在机制。
> 
> 方法：使用Tsc1CKO小鼠，通过视频、脑电图（EEG）和肌电图分析研究癫痫发作与睡眠的关系，包括发作频率对觉醒状态（觉醒、快速眼动[REM]睡眠、非快速眼动[NREM]睡眠）的依赖性，以及发作对觉醒-睡眠周期的影响。通过免疫组化评估下丘脑中食欲素的表达及其与发作频率的关系。利用视频脑电图测试食欲素拮抗剂对睡眠和癫痫发作的影响。
> 
> 结果：在Tsc1CKO小鼠中，癫痫发作总数在非快速眼动（NREM）睡眠中最常见，但在对各觉醒状态的时间占比进行标准化后，REM睡眠中的发作频率最高。相反，癫痫发作与觉醒-睡眠周期的破坏有关，特别是REM睡眠的减少。与对照组小鼠或无发作的Tsc1CKO小鼠相比，有发作的Tsc1CKO小鼠下丘脑食欲素表达增加。食欲素拮抗剂苏沃雷生（suvorexant）逆转了REM睡眠的减少，但对Tsc1CKO小鼠的癫痫发作无显著影响。
> 
> 意义：本研究在TSC小鼠模型中证实了癫痫发作与睡眠之间的双向关系，令人惊讶的是，相对发作频率在REM睡眠中最高，且发作会导致REM睡眠中断。下丘脑食欲素可能在一定程度上介导了这些癫痫-睡眠相互作用，食欲素拮抗剂可能成为治疗TSC睡眠障碍的合理疗法。

### 第二部分 AI 大师评价

## 2. 抗富亮氨酸胶质瘤失活蛋白1（LGI1）脑炎患者认知结局背后的海马萎缩与功能塑性及其临床相关性研究

**原文链接：** [点击跳转](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41674472)

**PMID：** 41674472
**DOI：** 10.1002/epi.70141

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** Hippocampal atrophy and functional plasticity underlie cognitive outcome in anti-leucine-rich glioma-inactivated protein 1 encephalitis and their clinical correlates.

> **英文摘要：**
> OBJECTIVE: Patients with anti-leucine-rich glioma-inactivated protein 1 (LGI1) encephalitis frequently exhibit long-term cognitive impairment despite immunotherapy. In this study, we aimed to delineate hippocampal structural and functional alterations that underlie these deficits and examined their clinical correlates. METHODS: We recruited 34 patients with anti-LGI1 encephalitis in the post-acute phase and 34 matched healthy controls. All participants underwent neuropsychological testing, high-resolution T1-weighted magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), and resting-state functional MRI. We assessed group differences in hippocampal volume and its whole-brain functional connectivity (FC) using a seed-based approach. Partial correlation, multivariable linear regression, and mediation analyses were employed to relate imaging metrics to cognitive scores and clinical features. RESULTS: Patients exhibited significant cognitive impairment, predominantly in verbal memory. This was paralleled by bilateral hippocampal atrophy, which strongly correlated with poorer performance across multiple cognitive domains. In contrast, patients demonstrated significantly increased FC between the left hippocampus and medial orbitofrontal cortex (mOFC). The enhanced connectivity was associated with better memory performance, suggesting a compensatory mechanism. Mediation analyses revealed that ipsilateral hippocampal volume mediated the relationship between acute medial temporal T2/fluid-attenuated inversion recovery (FLAIR) hyperintensity and memory scores. In addition, early immunotherapy was associated with an increase in left hippocampus-mOFC connectivity, contributing to improved cognitive performance. SIGNIFICANCE: Our findings reveal a dual neural mechanism underlying cognitive outcome in anti-LGI1 encephalitis: the hippocampal atrophy correlates with cognitive deficits, whereas enhanced left hippocampal-mOFC connectivity represents a compensatory plastic response. Early immunotherapy may promote this beneficial plasticity, highlighting these structural and functional signatures as potential biomarkers for stratifying patients and monitoring therapeutic efficacy.

> **中文摘要：**
> 目的：尽管接受了免疫治疗，抗富亮氨酸胶质瘤失活蛋白1（LGI1）脑炎患者仍经常表现出长期认知障碍。在本研究中，我们旨在描绘导致这些缺陷的海马结构和功能改变，并探讨其临床相关性。方法：我们招募了34名处于急性期后的抗LGI1脑炎患者和34名匹配的健康对照者。所有参与者均接受了神经心理学测试、高分辨率T1加权磁共振成像（MRI）和静息态功能MRI。我们使用种子点方法评估了组间海马体积及其全脑功能连接（FC）的差异。采用偏相关分析、多变量线性回归和中介分析将影像指标与认知评分及临床特征联系起来。结果：患者表现出显著的认知障碍，主要集中在言语记忆方面。这与双侧海马萎缩相平行，且萎缩与多个认知领域的较差表现强相关。相比之下，患者表现出左侧海马与内侧眶额皮层（mOFC）之间的FC显著增加。增强的连接性与更好的记忆表现相关，提示一种补偿机制。中介分析显示，同侧海马体积在中介急性期内侧颞叶T2/液体衰减反转恢复（FLAIR）高信号与记忆评分之间的关系中起作用。此外，早期免疫治疗与左侧海马-mOFC连接性的增加相关，有助于改善认知表现。意义：我们的研究结果揭示了抗LGI1脑炎认知结局背后的双重神经机制：海马萎缩与认知缺陷相关，而左侧海马-mOFC连接性增强则代表了一种补偿性的塑性反应。早期免疫治疗可能促进这种有益的塑性，凸显了这些结构和功能特征作为分层患者和监测治疗疗效的潜在生物标志物的价值。

### 第二部分 AI 大师评价

本研究深入探讨了抗LGI1脑炎患者长期认知障碍的神经生物学机制，创新性地提出了海马萎缩（损伤）与海马-mOFC环路增强（补偿）共存的双重病理生理模型。研究不仅明确了海马体积在介导急性期病变与长期记忆损伤中的关键作用，还首次证实了早期免疫治疗对脑功能补偿性塑性的促进作用。该发现为临床预测认知预后提供了量化的多模态影像学指标，具有较高的临床转化价值，但未来仍需通过大样本纵向研究进一步验证其动态演变过程。
