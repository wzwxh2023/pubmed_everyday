## 5. 成人肥胖与严重感染风险：一项多队列研究及全球负担估算

**原文链接：** [点击跳转](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41679324)
**期刊：** Lancet (London, England)
**PMID：** 41679324
**DOI：** 10.1016/S0140-6736(25)02474-2

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** Adult obesity and risk of severe infections: a multicohort study with global burden estimates.

> **英文摘要：**
> BACKGROUND: Adult obesity has been linked to specific infections, but evidence across the full spectrum of infectious diseases remains scarce. In this multicohort study with impact modelling, we examined the association between this preventable risk factor and the incidence, hospitalisations, and mortality of 925 bacterial, viral, parasitic, and fungal infectious diseases, and estimated their global and regional attributable impact. METHODS: We used pooled data from two Finnish cohort studies and repeated analyses in an independent population from the UK Biobank. BMI was assessed at baseline (1998-2002 in the Finnish studies; 2006-10 in UK Biobank), and participants were categorised as having healthy weight (18·5-24·9 kg/m), overweight (25·0-29·9 kg/m) or obesity, classified as class I (30·0-34·9 kg/m), class II (35·0-39·9 kg/m), or class III (≥40·0 kg/m). Participants were followed up through national hospitalisation and mortality registries for hospital admissions and deaths due to infectious diseases. Using hazard ratios derived from the Finnish cohorts and UK Biobank, along with obesity prevalence estimates from the Global Burden of Diseases, Injuries, and Risk Factors Study database, we estimated the proportion of fatal infections attributable to obesity globally, regionally, and by country for the years 2018 (before), 2021 (during), and 2023 (after the COVID-19 pandemic). FINDINGS: The analysis included 67 766 adults (mean age 42·1 [SD 10·8] years; 49 516 [73·1%] females, 18 250 [26·9%] males) from the Finnish cohorts and 479 498 adults (mean age 57·0 [SD 8·1] years; 261 084 [54·4%] females, 218 414 [45·6%] males) from UK Biobank. Participants had no recent history of infection-related hospitalisations at baseline. During follow-up, there were 8230 incident infection cases in the Finnish cohorts and 81 945 in UK Biobank. Compared with individuals of healthy weight, those with class III obesity had a three-times higher risk of infection-related hospital admissions (Finnish cohorts 2·75 [95% CI 2·24-3·37], UK Biobank 3·07 [2·95-3·19]), death (Finnish cohorts 3·06 [1·25-7·49], UK Biobank 3·54 [3·15-3·98]), or either outcome (Finnish cohorts 2·69 [2·19-3·30], UK Biobank 3·07 [2·95-3·19]). The corresponding pooled hazard ratio for either fatal or non-fatal severe infection among individuals with any obesity (classes I-III) was 1·7 (1·7-1·8). This association was consistent across different indicators of obesity (BMI, waist circumference, and waist-to-height ratio), demographic and clinical subgroups, and a wide range of infections (non-fatal and fatal, acute and chronic, bacterial and viral [including subtypes], and parasitic and fungal). Applying these risk estimates to global burden of disease data, the population attributable fractions of infection-related deaths due to obesity were estimated at 8·6% (6·6-11·1) in 2018, 15·0% (12·8-17·4) in 2021, and 10·8% (8·6-13·6) in 2023. INTERPRETATION: Adult obesity is a risk factor for infection-related hospitalisations and mortality across diverse pathogen types, populations, and baseline clinical profiles, with evidence suggesting that approximately one in ten infection-related deaths worldwide might be attributable to obesity.

> **中文摘要：**
> 【背景】成人肥胖已与特定感染相关联，但针对全谱系感染性疾病的证据仍然匮乏。在这项结合影响建模的多队列研究中，我们探讨了这一可预防风险因素与925种细菌、病毒、寄生虫和真菌感染性疾病的发病率、住院率及死亡率之间的关联，并估算了其在全球和区域层面的归因影响。
> 
> 【方法】我们使用了两项芬兰队列研究的汇总数据，并在来自英国生物样本库（UK Biobank）的独立人群中进行了重复分析。基线时（芬兰研究为1998-2002年；英国生物样本库为2006-10年）评估BMI，并将受试者分为健康体重（18.5-24.9 kg/m²）、超重（25.0-29.9 kg/m²）或肥胖（分为I级：30.0-34.9 kg/m²；II级：35.0-39.9 kg/m²；III级：≥40.0 kg/m²）。通过国家住院和死亡登记系统追踪受试者因感染性疾病住院和死亡的情况。利用芬兰队列和英国生物样本库得出的风险比，结合全球疾病负担研究（GBD）数据库的肥胖患病率估值，我们估算了2018年（新冠前）、2021年（新冠期间）和2023年（新冠后）全球、区域及国家层面归因于肥胖的致命性感染比例。
> 
> 【结果】分析纳入了来自芬兰队列的67,766名成人和来自英国生物样本库的479,498名成人。受试者在基线时均无近期感染相关住院史。随访期间，芬兰队列发生8,230例感染病例，英国生物样本库发生81,945例。与健康体重者相比，III级肥胖者的感染相关住院风险（芬兰队列 2.75 [95% CI 2.24-3.37]，英国生物样本库 3.07 [2.95-3.19]）和死亡风险（芬兰队列 3.06 [1.25-7.49]，英国生物样本库 3.54 [3.15-3.98]）均高出三倍。所有肥胖等级（I-III级）合并后的致死或非致死严重感染风险比为1.7 (1.7-1.8)。这种关联在不同肥胖指标、亚组及广泛的感染类型中均保持一致。基于全球疾病负担数据的建模估算显示，2018年、2021年和2023年全球归因于肥胖的感染相关死亡的人口归因分值（PAF）分别为8.6%、15.0%和10.8%。
> 
> 【解释】成人肥胖是跨病原体类型、人群和基线临床特征的感染相关住院和死亡的重要风险因素。证据表明，全球约十分之一的感染相关死亡可能归因于肥胖。

### 第二部分 AI 大师评价

本研究通过整合芬兰队列与英国生物样本库的超大规模数据，深入探讨了肥胖与多达925种感染性疾病的量化关联，具有极高的统计学效力。研究揭示了重度肥胖与感染住院及死亡风险之间显著的线性相关，并通过全球疾病负担建模，首次提出了约1/10的感染相关死亡可归因于肥胖。该研究极大地拓展了我们对肥胖公共卫生危害的理解，不仅证明了其在慢性病中的影响，更强调了肥胖在全病原体防御中的关键地位。其创新性在于多维度指标验证和宏观负担估算，为全球性减重干预以降低传染病风险提供了有力的流行病学证据。
