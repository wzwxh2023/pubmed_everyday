## 1. PLA2G16介导的二十四碳四烯酸通过重塑脂肪酸氧化损害CD8 T细胞免疫功能，从而促进乳腺癌肺转移

**原文链接：** [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41241920](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41241920)
**期刊：** Advanced science (Weinheim, Baden-Wurttemberg, Germany)
**PMID：** 41241920
**DOI：** 10.1002/advs.202510224

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** PLA2G16-Mediated Tetracosatetraenoic Acid Rewires Fatty Acid Oxidation to Impair CD8 T Cell Immune Function in Promoting Breast Cancer Lung Metastasis.

**英文摘要原文：**
Tumor-related metabolites in the tumor microenvironment may induce immune dysfunction, leading to malignant progression and metastasis of tumors. Here, it is demonstrated that tumoral PLA2G16, a phospholipase catalyzes phospholipids to generate free fatty acid (FFA) or lysophosphatidic acid (LPA), is an important contributor to triple-negative breast cancer (TNBC) lung metastasis in an immune-dependent pattern by improving tetracosatetraenoic acid (C24:4 (n-6)) accumulation in the early metastatic niche of lung and impairing immune function of pulmonary CD8 T cells. C24:4 (n-6) induces nuclear import of PPARα in pneumal CD8 T cells, which regulates the transcription of Cpt1a, Dgat1, Cd36, and Fabp1, leading to the activation of fatty acid oxidation (FAO). The robust FAO results in suppression of CD8 T cells. Genetically depleting PPARα in mice, pharmacologically inhibiting C24:4 (n-6)-induced PPARα in the nucleus or directly suppressing PPARα activity effectively attenuates PLA2G16-C24:4 (n-6) axis-based immune dysfunction of CD8 T cells and their according anti-tumor activities. These results imply that PLA2G16-mediated C24:4 (n-6) accumulation in the lung acts as a metabolic disorder to CD8 T cell antitumor activity and highlights a critical role of PLA2G16 in promoting TNBC lung metastasis. Targeting PLA2G16 and combination with anti-PD-1-based immunotherapy may be an effective strategy for clinical tumor immunotherapy.

**中文摘要译文：**
肿瘤微环境中的肿瘤相关代谢物可能诱导免疫功能障碍，从而导致肿瘤的恶性进展和转移。本研究证明，肿瘤来源的PLA2G16（一种催化磷脂生成游离脂肪酸（FFA）或溶血磷脂酸（LPA）的磷脂酶）通过增加肺部早期转移微环境中二十四碳四烯酸（C24:4 (n-6)）的积累，并损害肺部CD8 T细胞的免疫功能，以免疫依赖的方式成为三阴性乳腺癌（TNBC）肺转移的重要促进因素。C24:4 (n-6)诱导肺部CD8 T细胞中PPARα的核内转位，后者调控Cpt1a、Dgat1、Cd36和Fabp1的转录，从而激活脂肪酸氧化（FAO）。增强的脂肪酸氧化导致CD8 T细胞功能受到抑制。在小鼠中通过基因手段敲除PPARα、药物抑制C24:4 (n-6)诱导的PPARα核内转位或直接抑制PPARα活性，均可有效减弱基于PLA2G16-C24:4 (n-6)轴的CD8 T细胞免疫功能障碍及其相应的抗肿瘤活性。这些结果表明，PLA2G16介导的肺内C24:4 (n-6)积累作为一种代谢紊乱因素，损害了CD8 T细胞的抗肿瘤活性，并凸显了PLA2G16在促进TNBC肺转移中的关键作用。靶向PLA2G16并联合基于抗PD-1的免疫疗法可能成为一种有效的临床肿瘤免疫治疗策略。

### 第二部分 AI 大师评价

该研究旨在阐明肿瘤源性PLA2G16在三阴性乳腺癌（TNBC）肺转移中的作用机制。研究发现，肿瘤细胞分泌的PLA2G16能够提高肺转移微环境中二十四碳四烯酸（C24:4）的水平，后者通过激活CD8 T细胞内的PPARα信号通路，重塑其代谢模式为脂肪酸氧化，从而抑制了T细胞的抗肿瘤免疫功能。这项研究的创新之处在于揭示了一条连接肿瘤代谢、特定脂肪酸分子与免疫抑制的关键信号轴，为预防和治疗TNBC肺转移提供了靶向PLA2G16的新策略，并提示其与PD-1抑制剂联合应用的潜力。

## 2. 闪速光动力疗法：光敏剂吸收饱和如何实现选择性及更深层的肿瘤治疗

**原文链接：** [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41241814](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41241814)
**期刊：** Advanced science (Weinheim, Baden-Wurttemberg, Germany)
**PMID：** 41241814
**DOI：** 10.1002/advs.202513199

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** Flash Photodynamic Therapy - How the Saturation of Photosensitizer Absorption Enables Selective and Deeper Tumor Treatments.

**英文摘要原文：**
FLASH therapies are attracting tremendous interest because they spare normal tissues while maintaining tumor-destroying efficacy, when compared with continuous delivery of radiation. In Photodynamic Therapy (PDT), it has been noted that continuous-wave and pulsed lasers give comparable results for a variety of tumors, but the conditions for increased tumor-to-peritumoral tissue selectivity have never been reported. This work presents a model that explains how pulsed lasers, in combination with photosensitizers, can offer selective and in-depth tumor ablation. It is shown that the photosensitizer absorption must be saturated to obtain the FLASH effect. The importance of the number of laser pulses to destroy tumor tissue and spare normal tissue is demonstrated. The predictions of the model and the superiority of FLASH-PDT are validated with the treatment of subcutaneous CT26 and orthotopic 4T1 tumors models. Notably, FLASH-PDT with redaporfin significantly increases the overall survival of mice with 4 mm orthotopic 4T1 tumors and lung metastasis. FLASH-PDT allows for the use of order-of-magnitude higher drug or light doses without affecting healthy tissues, while promoting selective and deeper tumor treatments.

## 3. KLF5通过AAA+ ATP酶共激活因子RUVBL1和RUVBL2驱动胰腺癌的双向谱系分化程序。

**原文链接：** [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41241675](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41241675)
**期刊：** Nature communications
**PMID：** 41241675
**DOI：** 10.1038/s41467-025-66007-0

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** KLF5 enables dichotomous lineage programs in pancreatic cancer via the AAA+ ATPase coactivators RUVBL1 and RUVBL2.

**英文摘要原文：**
Lineage plasticity is a hallmark of pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC) and contributes to tumor heterogeneity and therapeutic resistance. Here, we identify KLF5 as a dynamic master regulator of epithelial lineage identity in PDAC, with dichotomous roles in promoting either classical or basal-like transcriptional programs. Through unbiased proteomic and genetic screens, we uncover the AAA+ ATPases RUVBL1 and RUVBL2 as essential coactivators of KLF5 across both lineage states. We demonstrate that ATP hydrolysis by RUVBL1/2 is required for the stable interaction with an intrinsically disordered region of KLF5, enabling its recruitment to lineage-specific enhancers and driving transcriptional regulation of identity-defining genes. Notably, small-molecule inhibitors of RUVBL1/2 ATPase activity, which have anti-PDAC activity in vivo, suppress KLF5-dependent transcription. These findings define a previously unrecognized mechanism of ATP hydrolysis-dependent transcriptional coactivation and highlight a potential therapeutic strategy for modulating aberrant lineage programs in cancer.

**中文摘要译文：**
谱系可塑性是胰腺导管腺癌（PDAC）的一个标志性特征，并导致了肿瘤的异质性和治疗抵抗。在此，我们发现KLF5是PDAC中上皮谱系特征的一个动态主调控因子，在促进经典型或基底样转录程序中扮演着双重角色。通过无偏见的蛋白质组学和遗传学筛选，我们揭示了AAA+ ATP酶RUVBL1和RUVBL2是KLF5在两种谱系状态下都必需的共激活因子。我们证明，RUVBL1/2的ATP水解作用是其与KLF5的一个内在无序区稳定相互作用所必需的，这使其能够被招募到谱系特异性增强子上，并驱动决定细胞身份的基因的转录调控。值得注意的是，在体内具有抗PDAC活性的RUVBL1/2 ATP酶活性小分子抑制剂，能够抑制KLF5依赖的转录过程。这些发现阐明了一种前所未识的ATP水解依赖性转录共激活机制，并凸显了一种调节癌症中异常谱系程序的潜在治疗策略。

### 第二部分 AI 大师评价

本研究旨在阐明转录因子KLF5在胰腺癌谱系可塑性中的关键作用，并运用蛋白质组学和遗传学筛选等方法探究其调控机制。研究关键发现KLF5能双向调控胰腺癌的经典型与基底样两种谱系，且其功能依赖于AAA+ ATP酶RUVBL1/2作为共激活因子，这种相互作用需要RUVBL1/2的ATP水解活性。这项工作不仅揭示了一种前所未有的ATP水解依赖性转录共激活机制，更重要的是，它指出了靶向RUVBL1/2 ATP酶活性是抑制胰腺癌谱系可塑性、克服治疗抵抗的一种极具前景的治疗新策略。
