## 3. 重新定义志贺毒素诱导的人类细胞死亡为 NLRP1 和 Gasdermin E 介导的细胞焦亡

**原文链接：** [点击跳转](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41533439)
**期刊：** Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences of the United States of America
**PMID：** 41533439
**DOI：** 10.1073/pnas.2510274123

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** Redefining Shiga toxin-induced human cell death as NLRP1- and gasdermin E-mediated pyroptosis.

> **英文摘要：**
> Shiga toxin (Stx)-mediated hemolytic uremic syndrome (HUS) prevails as the leading cause of pediatric renal failure worldwide despite decades of efforts to develop therapeutic strategies. Stx killing of large populations of sensitive cells in the vasculature and kidney underlies HUS development. However, the exact nature of Stx-induced cell death and its mechanism are not clear. Here, we demonstrate that Stx-induced cell death in several HUS-relevant human cells, such as kidney epithelial cells, podocytes, and human intestinal microvascular endothelial cells, is pyroptosis, an inflammatory form of cell death. Remarkably, our findings identify gasdermin E (GSDME) activation as the cardinal event that mediates Stx killing of human cells. Mechanistically, Stx activates, through ribotoxic stress, a caspase-8-caspase-3 pathway that licenses GSDME-dependent pyroptosis of susceptible cells. Intriguingly, NLRP1 amplifies this pyroptotic pathway in certain Stx-sensitive cells by promoting caspase-8 activation. Together, our findings define the nature and mechanism of a bacterial toxin-induced cell death, providing crucial insights into pathogenic determinants of a critical pediatric illness.

> **中文摘要：**
> 尽管数十年来一直致力于开发治疗策略，但志贺毒素（Stx）介导的溶血性尿毒症综合征（HUS）仍是全球儿童肾衰竭的主要原因。Stx 杀伤血管系统和肾脏中的大量敏感细胞是 HUS 发生的基础。然而，Stx 诱导的细胞死亡的确切性质及其机制尚不清楚。在这里，我们证明了在几种 HUS 相关的人类细胞（如肾上皮细胞、足细胞和人类肠道微血管内皮细胞）中，Stx 诱导的细胞死亡是细胞焦亡，这是一种炎症形式的细胞死亡。值得注意的是，我们的研究结果确定了 Gasdermin E (GSDME) 的激活是介导 Stx 杀伤人类细胞的核心事件。从机制上讲，Stx 通过核糖毒性应激激活 caspase-8-caspase-3 通路，从而允许敏感细胞发生 GSDME 依赖性细胞焦亡。有趣的是，NLRP1 通过促进 caspase-8 激活，在某些 Stx 敏感细胞中放大这一焦亡通路。总之，我们的研究结果定义了一种细菌毒素诱导细胞死亡的性质和机制，为这种严重儿科疾病的致病因素提供了关键见解。

### 第二部分 AI 大师评价

该研究挑战了志贺毒素诱导细胞凋亡的传统认识，明确其核心致死机制为 GSDME 介导的细胞焦亡。研究揭示了由核糖毒性应激触发的 Caspase-8-Caspase-3 通路是驱动焦亡的关键，并发现了 NLRP1 在这一过程中的放大效应。这一发现为溶血性尿毒症综合征（HUS）的病理生理学提供了全新的分子解释，并为开发针对细胞焦亡通路的干预策略提供了理论依据。

## 4. 生态级联效应将气候变率与鸟类爆发性迁徙及人畜共患沙门氏菌病疫情联系起来

**原文链接：** [点击跳转](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41525483)
**期刊：** Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences of the United States of America
**PMID：** 41525483
**DOI：** 10.1073/pnas.2511209123

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** An ecological cascade links climatic variability to avian irruptions and zoonotic salmonellosis outbreaks.

> **英文摘要：**
> Salmonellosis is a common bacterial infection that can lead to severe illness and death in humans as well as domesticated and wild animals. In bird populations, salmonellosis outbreaks are temporally irregular but occur simultaneously across large spatial extents, frequently leading to widespread avian mortality with occasional spillover to humans. Here, we test the hypothesis that avian salmonellosis epidemics result from a cascade of ecological processes triggered by climate variability in coniferous forests. Building on well-evidenced ecological mechanisms, we find strong evidence for multiyear causal links between temperature cues that drive substantial "boom-bust" cycles in tree cone production across North America, the subsequent irruptions of seed-eating birds, and, finally, the eventual occurrence and severity of avian salmonellosis outbreaks in the United States. By using an integrated, multilevel Bayesian statistical model as a predictive tool to forecast future bird irruptions and disease outbreaks, we demonstrate that our methods can be employed as an early warning system for future epidemics, potentially mitigating outbreak severity among birds and lowering the risk of zoonotic spillover events by encouraging measures such as the temporary cessation of bird feeding ahead of high-risk periods.

> **中文摘要：**
> 沙门氏菌病是一种常见的细菌感染，可导致人类以及家畜和野生动物出现严重疾病甚至死亡。在鸟类种群中，沙门氏菌病的爆发在时间上是不规律的，但在广阔的地理范围内同时发生，经常导致大规模的鸟类死亡，并偶尔向人类溢出。在本研究中，我们测试了这样一个假设：鸟类沙门氏菌病疫情源于针叶林气候变率触发的一系列生态过程级联。基于证据充分的生态机制，我们发现了强有力的证据，表明在驱动整个北美树木球果产量出现大幅“繁荣-萧条”周期的温度信号、随后的食籽鸟类爆发性迁徙，以及最终在美国发生的鸟类沙门氏菌病疫情的发生与严重程度之间，存在跨越多年的因果联系。通过使用综合的多级贝叶斯统计模型作为预测未来鸟类迁徙和疾病爆发的工具，我们证明了我们的方法可以作为未来流行病的早期预警系统，通过鼓励在高风险期之前临时停止喂鸟等措施，潜在地减轻鸟类中的疫情严重程度并降低人畜共患溢出事件的风险。

### 第二部分 AI 大师评价

该研究通过构建跨营养级的生态级联模型，成功揭示了气候波动、植物产籽周期与鸟类传染病爆发之间的深度关联。其核心创新在于利用多级贝叶斯统计工具，将宏观的气候信号转化为可预测的公共卫生预警指标，为理解人畜共患病的生态驱动力提供了有力证据。研究不仅在理论上阐明了环境变量如何通过生物链条放大疾病风险，在实践上也为降低人畜共患病溢出风险提供了具体的预防性干预策略，具有极高的公共卫生价值。
