## 2. Smc5/6 与 DNA 连接处结合的分子决定因素

**原文链接：** [点击跳转](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41547900)
**期刊：** Nature communications
**PMID：** 41547900
**DOI：** 10.1038/s41467-025-67999-5

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** Molecular determinants of Smc5/6 association with DNA junctions.

> **英文摘要：**
> Smc5/6 is an essential genome maintenance complex that interacts with double-stranded (ds) DNA, single-stranded (ss) DNA, and ss-dsDNA junctions. DNA association underlies Smc5/6's functions in managing intermediates generated during genome replication and repair. However, the mechanisms of this activity are not fully understood. Here, we report a single-molecule study examining Smc5/6 association with a dsDNA substrate containing a ssDNA gap with defined 3' and 5' junctions. We found that Smc5/6 associates with both 3' and 5' junctions but prefers the 3' junction in the presence of the ssDNA-binding complex RPA. Further, Smc5/6's junction association frequency and dwell time are regulated by two non-SMC subcomplexes and DNA binding residues of Smc6. Moreover, Smc5/6 prefers binding to junction sites free of the sliding clamp PCNA over those occupied with it. These results suggest that Smc5/6 utilizes its multiple structural modules to associate with junction sites in coordination with other genome maintenance factors.

> **中文摘要：**
> Smc5/6 是一种核心的基因组维持复合物，可与双链 (ds) DNA、单链 (ss) DNA 以及 ss-dsDNA 连接处相互作用。DNA 结合是 Smc5/6 在管理基因组复制和修复过程中产生的中间体方面发挥功能的基础。然而，这一活动的机制尚未完全阐明。在此，我们报告了一项单分子研究，考察了 Smc5/6 与含有明确 3' 和 5' 连接处的 ssDNA 缺口 dsDNA 底物的结合情况。我们发现 Smc5/6 与 3' 和 5' 连接处均有结合，但在单链 DNA 结合复合物 RPA 存在的情况下，它更倾向于结合 3' 连接处。此外，Smc5/6 的连接处结合频率和停留时间受到两个非 SMC 亚复合物以及 Smc6 DNA 结合残基的调节。此外，Smc5/6 倾向于结合不含滑动夹 PCNA 的连接位点，而非被其占据的位点。这些结果表明，Smc5/6 利用其多个结构模块，与其他基因组维持因子协调配合，与连接位点结合。

**英文原标题：** Robust characterization and interpretation of rare pathogenic cell populations from spatial omics using GARDEN.

> **英文摘要：**
> Spatial omics links molecular measurements to their positions in tissue, revealing cellular organization and interactions. Yet most computational tools highlight common cell types and overlook rare populations that can drive disease. Here we show GARDEN, a computational framework that identifies and characterizes these pathogenic cells or regions in spatial omics by embedding graph-based dynamic attention into a spatially-aware graph fusion contrastive model. In an Alzheimer's disease model, GARDEN localizes C1qa/C1qb-marked microglia in amyloid-β regions and reveals key immune pathways. In nasopharyngeal carcinoma it identifies tiny tertiary lymphoid structures, and in breast cancer it uncovers inflammatory M1-like macrophages near ductal carcinoma in situ and links them to pro-metastatic signaling. An interpretation module pinpoints key immune signatures, and GARDEN extends to spatial chromatin accessibility, providing insight into epigenetic regulation and informing diagnostics and therapeutic targeting.

> **中文摘要：**
> 空间组学将分子测量结果与其在组织中的位置联系起来，揭示了细胞的组织结构和相互作用。然而，大多数计算工具侧重于突出常见细胞类型，而忽视了可能驱动疾病的稀有细胞群。在此，我们展示了 GARDEN，这是一个计算框架，通过将基于图的动态注意力嵌入到空间感知图融合对比模型中，在空间组学中识别并表征这些致病细胞或区域。GARDEN 在不同组织、物种和分辨率尺度上表现一致，并能对齐连续切片以重建三维解剖结构。在阿尔茨海默病模型中，GARDEN 定位了淀粉样蛋白-β 区域中以 C1qa/C1qb 为标记的小胶质细胞，并揭示了关键的免疫通路。在鼻咽癌中，它识别出微小的三级淋巴结构；在乳腺癌中，它发现了原位导管癌附近的炎症性 M1 样巨噬细胞，并将其与促转移信号联系起来。一个解释模块精确指出了关键的免疫特征，且 GARDEN 可扩展至空间染色质可及性研究，为表观遗传调控提供见解，并为诊断和治疗靶向提供信息。

> **英文摘要：**
> Endoplasmic reticulum-associated degradation (ERAD) is a critical protein quality control mechanism that also regulates lipid metabolism and calcium homeostasis. Dysregulation of ERAD and unfolded protein response underlies diseases including cancer, neurodegenerative disorders, and metabolic syndromes. Small molecule modulators of ERAD could enable mechanistic discovery and therapeutic intervention, but few have been identified. Using a high-content screening, we discovered several ERAD-modulating compounds, including NCATS-SM0225, an ERAD inhibitor that unexpectedly binds all three isoforms of VDAC, outer mitochondrial membrane proteins enriched at mitochondria-associated membranes. > **英文摘要：**
> Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) is a leading global health concern, recognized for its complex pathogenesis and high mortality rates. The metastatic progression of HCC, considered the terminal event in tumor development, plays a pivotal role in determining patient prognosis, with metastasis being a key factor in poor survival outcomes.HDAC11 was found to be highly expressed in HCC tissues, with its elevated expression significantly correlating with poor patient survival.
