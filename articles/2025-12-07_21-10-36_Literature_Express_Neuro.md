## 1. 母体高钙血症导致的新生儿低钙血症：临床特征、病因、治疗与预后

**原文链接：** [点击跳转](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41353515)
**期刊：** Pediatric research
**PMID：** 41353515
**DOI：** 10.1038/s41390-025-04577-x

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** Neonatal hypocalcemia caused by maternal hypercalcemia: clinical characteristics, etiology, treatment, and outcome.

> **英文摘要：**
> BACKGROUND: Maternal hypercalcemia is a rare but potentially serious cause of late-onset neonatal hypocalcemia. Data on the clinical characteristics and outcomes of mother-baby couplets with maternal hypercalcemia and subsequent neonatal hypocalcemia remains scarce.
> 
> METHODS: This retrospective study reviewed medical records of neonates with late-onset hypocalcemia (≥3 days after birth) caused by maternal hypercalcemia at a tertiary medical center in Taiwan from 2014 to 2021. A stepwise diagnostic approach was applied to exclude other secondary causes. The etiologies of maternal hypercalcemia were assessed. Clinical outcomes and treatment responses of the mother-infant dyads were extracted from follow-up records documented during routine clinical care.
> 
> RESULTS: A total of seven neonates (6 males, 1 female) were documented. All affected neonates presented with seizure between 5 and 12 days of age. Laboratory findings revealed hypocalcemia (median: 5.3 mg/dL), hyperphosphatemia (median: 10.2 mg/dL), inappropriately intact parathyroid hormone (median: 18.4 pg/mL), and low 25-hydroxyvitamin D levels. Neonates born to mothers with severe hypercalcemia (≥11 mg/dL) had lower serum calcium and higher phosphorus levels compared to those with maternal calcium <11 mg/dL. For all neonates, hypocalcemia and hyperphosphatemia normalized within one month with calcium and 1,25 (OH) Vit. D supplement. During a median 2-year follow-up period, there were no recurrent hypocalcemia cases. One child had delayed speech. Maternal evaluation revealed functional parathyroid adenomas in six of seven (86%) mothers; four underwent parathyroidectomy; two received conservative treatment. One mother with a CASR mutation achieved normocalcemia with calcimimetics. All mothers recovered without serious sequelae.
> 
> CONCLUSIONS: Late-onset neonatal hypocalcemia should prompt maternal calcium evaluation even in asymptomatic mothers. Maternal total serum calcium levels ≥11 mg/dL may require more intensive neonatal monitoring and prolonged treatment. Genetic testing should be considered in mother with negative result of parathyroid scan.
> 
> IMPACT: This study provides the first comprehensive analysis of maternal hypercalcemia-induced neonatal hypocalcemia, identifying functional parathyroid adenomas as the predominant cause (86%). Maternal calcium levels ≥11 mg/dL correlate with more severe neonatal hypocalcemia, establishing evidence-based risk stratification for clinical management. Emphasizes routine maternal calcium screening in late-onset neonatal hypocalcemia and genetic testing when parathyroid imaging is negative. Demonstrates excellent prognosis with complete biochemical normalization in all neonates within three months and minimal long-term neurodevelopmental sequelae.

> **中文摘要：**
> 背景：母体高钙血症是一种罕见但潜在严重的迟发性新生儿低钙血症的病因。关于母体高钙血症伴随继发新生儿低钙血症的母婴配对临床特征及预后资料仍十分有限。
> 
> 方法：本回顾性研究分析了2014年至2021年期间于台湾一所三级医疗中心收治的迟发性低钙血症（出生后≥3天）且由母体高钙血症引起的新生儿病历。采用分步诊断方法排除其他继发原因，并评估母体高钙血症的病因。从常规临床随访记录中提取母婴配对的临床结局与治疗反应。
> 
> 结果：共记录了7例新生儿（6男，1女）。所有患儿均在生后5至12天出现惊厥。实验室检查显示低钙血症（中位数：5.3 mg/dL）、高磷血症（中位数：10.2 mg/dL）、异常偏低的完整甲状旁腺激素水平（中位数：18.4 pg/mL）以及低25-羟维生素D水平。与母体血钙<11 mg/dL者相比，母体重度高钙血症（≥11 mg/dL）新生儿的血清钙水平更低、磷水平更高。所有新生儿在补充钙剂及1,25(OH)维生素D治疗下，于1个月内低钙血症与高磷血症均恢复正常。中位2年随访期间未出现复发病例，1例患儿出现语言发育迟缓。母体评估发现，7例母亲中有6例（86%）为功能性甲状旁腺腺瘤，其中4例接受甲状旁腺切除术，2例采取保守治疗。1例携带CASR基因突变的母亲应用钙受体模拟剂后恢复正常血钙。所有母亲均康复，无严重后遗症。
> 
> 结论：迟发性新生儿低钙血症应促使对母体血钙水平的评估，即便母亲无症状。母体总血钙≥11 mg/dL可能需要加强新生儿监测及延长治疗。若甲状旁腺影像学检查阴性，应考虑进行基因检测。
> 
> 研究影响：本研究首次系统分析了母体高钙血症导致的新生儿低钙血症，发现功能性甲状旁腺腺瘤是主要病因（86%）。母体血钙水平≥11 mg/dL与新生儿低钙血症的严重程度相关，为临床管理建立了基于证据的风险分层体系。研究强调，在迟发性新生儿低钙血症中需常规检测母体血钙，并在甲状旁腺影像阴性时考虑基因检测。所有新生儿在三个月内可达完全生化正常，长期神经发育后遗症极少，预后良好。

### 第二部分 AI 大师评价

该研究系统回顾了由母体高钙血症引起的新生儿迟发性低钙血症病例，是首个聚焦母婴配对的全面分析。结果揭示功能性甲状旁腺腺瘤为主要致病原因，并确证母体血钙水平≥11 mg/dL与新生儿病情严重程度相关。研究设计清晰、随访充分，凸显临床筛查与基因检测的重要性。尽管样本量有限，但为基于病因的风险评估与干预策略提供了重要实证依据。
