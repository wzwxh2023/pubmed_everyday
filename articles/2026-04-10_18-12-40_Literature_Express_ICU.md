> **英文摘要：**
> Mitochondrial transfer RNA (mt-tRNA) modification determines organelle translation and function. GTPBP3 and MTO1 catalyze 5-taurinomethyluridine (τmU) modification at wobble uridine of five mt-tRNAs. τmU hypomodification causes mitochondrial encephalomyopathy, but the underlying pathogenesis and intervention strategy due to GTPBP3 mutations are lacking. > **英文摘要：**
> RATIONALE: Chronic dyspnea and exercise impairment are common after acute pulmonary embolism but poorly characterised. OBJECTIVES: We performed a prospective observational study to validate an algorithm to diagnose discrete post-pulmonary embolism outcomes, including chronic thromboembolism phenotypes. METHODS: Six pulmonologists independently reviewed records from 150 consecutive patients three or more months after acute pulmonary embolism who had undergone a stepwise diagnostic algorithm (SEARCH): ymptom screen, cardiopulmonary xercise testing, pulmonary rterial perfusion scans, esting echocardiography, onfirmatory imaging, and emodynamic measurements by rest and exercise right heart catheterisation. MEASUREMENTS AND MAIN RESULTS: Symptomatic recovery was most common and occurred in 66.2% of patients during the final evaluation. The SEARCH protocol reliably identifies chronic thromboembolism phenotypes after acute embolism.

## 5. 减少腹部手术后肺部并发症的非药物围手术期干预措施：一项系统综述和荟萃分析。

**原文链接：** [点击跳转](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41956522)
**期刊：** BMJ (Clinical research ed.)
**PMID：** 41956522
**DOI：** 10.1136/bmj-2025-089001

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** Non-drug perioperative interventions to reduce postoperative pulmonary complications after abdominal surgery: systematic review and meta-analysis.

> **英文摘要：**
> OBJECTIVE: To evaluate the effectiveness of perioperative non-drug interventions in reducing postoperative pulmonary complications (PPCs) in adults undergoing abdominal surgery.
> 
> DESIGN: Systematic review and meta-analysis.
> 
> DATA SOURCES: Ovid MEDLINE, Embase, and Web of Science from database inception to January 2025 and updated in January 2026, with no language restrictions.
> 
> STUDY SELECTION: Randomised controlled trials assessing the effectiveness of perioperative non-drug interventions for the prevention of PPCs in adults undergoing elective abdominal surgery under general anaesthesia, with clearly defined PPCs.
> 
> MAIN OUTCOME MEASURES: The primary outcome was the proportion of patients developing PPCs. Secondary outcomes included the proportion of patients with PPC subtypes according to European Perioperative Clinical Outcome definitions (respiratory infection, respiratory failure, pleural effusion, atelectasis, or pneumothorax) and hospital length of stay.
> 
> DATA EXTRACTION AND SYNTHESIS: Two reviewers independently screened studies, extracted data, and assessed risk of bias with the Cochrane RoB 2.0 tool. Data were synthesised using meta-analyses and trial sequential analyses, with the evidence certainty assessed using the Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development and Evaluation (GRADE) approach.
> 
> RESULTS: 255 trials including 55 260 participants were included, evaluating 10 types of interventions with 39 subtypes for PPC prevention. PPCs occurred in 6467 (11.7%) participants across all included trials. High certainty evidence showed that low fraction of inspired oxygen (FiO) significantly reduced PPCs (risk ratio 0.81, 95% confidence interval 0.71 to 0.92). Moderate certainty evidence showed benefit for four intervention types: lung protective ventilation (risk ratio 0.66, 0.57 to 0.76), physiotherapy (0.55, 0.46 to 0.65), analgesia (0.73, 0.64 to 0.84), and nutrition (0.74, 0.63 to 0.87), with individualised positive end expiratory pressure, composite lung protective ventilation, early mobilisation, and epidural analgesia also showing benefit at the subtype level. Trial sequential analysis confirmed sufficient cumulative evidence for all the above interventions except early mobilisation. By contrast, goal directed haemodynamic therapy, targeted blood pressure management, restrictive fluid therapy, and postoperative bi-level positive airway pressure showed no evidence of benefit, with moderate certainty.
> 
> CONCLUSIONS: This synthesis establishes an evidence hierarchy for PPC prevention in abdominal surgery. Low FiO is the only intervention supported by high certainty evidence and should be prioritised in clinical practice. Other beneficial strategies include lung protective ventilation, physiotherapy, analgesic techniques, and nutrition interventions. Conversely, the role of goal directed haemodynamic therapy-despite its widespread use-warrants reconsideration for PPC prevention. These findings facilitate prioritisation of effective interventions and development of evidence based guidelines.
> 
> STUDY REGISTRATION: PROSPERO CRD42025637449.

> **中文摘要：**
> 目的：评估围手术期非药物干预措施在减少接受腹部手术的成年患者术后肺部并发症（PPCs）方面的有效性。
> 
> 设计：系统综述和荟萃分析。
> 
> 数据来源：Ovid MEDLINE、Embase 和 Web of Science，检索时间从数据库创建至2025年1月，并于2026年1月更新，无语言限制。
> 
> 研究筛选：纳入评估围手术期非药物干预措施对预防在全身麻醉下接受择期腹部手术且PPCs有明确定义的成年患者PPCs有效性的随机对照试验。
> 
> 主要结局指标：主要结局是发生PPCs的患者比例。次要结局包括根据欧洲围手术期临床结局定义出现PPC亚型（呼吸道感染、呼吸衰竭、胸腔积液、肺不张或气胸）的患者比例以及住院时间。
> 
> 数据提取与综合：两名评价员独立筛选研究、提取数据，并使用Cochrane RoB 2.0工具评估偏倚风险。数据通过荟萃分析和试验序贯分析进行综合，证据的确定性采用推荐分级、评估、制定与评价（GRADE）方法进行评估。
> 
> 结果：共纳入255项试验，包括55260名参与者，评估了10类、39种亚型的PPC预防干预措施。在所有纳入的试验中，共有6467名（11.7%）参与者发生PPCs。高确定性证据表明，低吸入氧浓度（FiO）显著降低了PPCs（风险比0.81，95%置信区间0.71至0.92）。中等确定性证据显示四类干预措施有益：肺保护性通气（风险比0.66，0.57至0.76）、物理治疗（0.55，0.46至0.65）、镇痛（0.73，0.64至0.84）和营养支持（0.74，0.63至0.87），在亚型层面，个体化呼气末正压、复合肺保护性通气、早期活动和硬膜外镇痛也显示出益处。试验序贯分析证实，除早期活动外，上述所有干预措施均有足够的累积证据。相比之下，目标导向血流动力学治疗、目标化血压管理、限制性液体治疗和术后双水平气道正压通气没有显示出获益的证据，证据确定性为中等。
> 
> 结论：本研究为腹部手术中预防PPC建立了一个证据等级体系。低吸入氧浓度是唯一有高确定性证据支持的干预措施，应在临床实践中优先考虑。其他有益的策略包括肺保护性通气、物理治疗、镇痛技术和营养干预。相反，尽管目标导向血流动力学治疗被广泛使用，其在预防PPC中的作用值得重新审视。这些发现有助于有效干预措施的优先排序和循证指南的制定。
> 
> 研究注册：PROSPERO CRD42025637449。

## 8. 肥胖 ICU 患者的机械通气：当前最佳实践与未来方向

**原文链接：** [点击跳转](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41954744)
**期刊：** Intensive care medicine
**PMID：** 41954744
**DOI：** 10.1007/s00134-026-08397-3

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** Mechanical ventilation for ICU patient with obesity: current best practices and future directions.

> **英文摘要：**
> Obesity is increasingly prevalent among critically ill patients and profoundly alters respiratory mechanics, gas exchange, and cardiopulmonary interactions, complicating ventilatory management. Excess adipose tissue increases pleural pressure, reduces functional residual capacity, and promotes airway closure and atelectasis, increasing susceptibility to hypoxemia, hypercapnia, and ventilator-induced lung injury. These physiological alterations necessitate tailored ventilatory strategies distinct from those used in patients without obesity. Lung-protective ventilation using tidal volume indexed to predicted body weight remains the cornerstone of management, as lung size does not increase proportionally with body mass. Patients with obesity often require higher positive end-expiratory pressure to counteract elevated pleural pressure and prevent airway closure although optimal titration strategies remain uncertain. Interpretation of airway pressures requires caution, as increased chest wall elastance may result in elevated plateau and driving pressures without excessive lung stress. Adjunctive monitoring tools, including esophageal pressure measurement and electrical impedance tomography, may help individualize ventilatory management by improving assessment of transpulmonary pressure and regional ventilation. Airway management is particularly challenging in patients with obesity due to rapid oxygen desaturation and increased risk of difficult intubation; positive-pressure preoxygenation and video laryngoscopy improve procedural safety.
