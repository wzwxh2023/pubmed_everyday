## 2. Lennox-Gastaut综合征的揭示：诊断、治疗与倡导的进展——来自热那亚国际研讨会的见解

**原文链接：** [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41159734](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41159734)
**期刊：** Epilepsia
**PMID：** 41159734
**DOI：** 10.1111/epi.18696

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** Lennox-Gastaut syndrome unveiled: Advancing diagnosis, therapies, and advocacy-insights from the Genoa International Workshop.

**英文摘要原文：**
Lennox-Gastaut syndrome (LGS) is one of the most severe, yet one of the most discussed, childhood-onset developmental and epileptic encephalopathies (DEEs). Dissent among epileptologists on the definition and minimum set of electroclinical features derives from the high etiological heterogeneity within the syndrome, which could make its prevalence overestimated. However, in recent years, our diagnostic strategies, including both high-resolution magnetic resonance imaging and next-generation sequencing techniques, have enabled us to disentangle many cases previously classified as "idiopathic." In addition, some electroencephalographic and circulating biomarkers have been identified that could predict disease progression and treatment response if confirmed in larger patient populations. As our diagnostic capacity increases, so do our treatment strategies. Although progress has been made, the implementation of better clinical trial designs, individualized treatments, and therapies that address the genetic roots of the disease remains necessary in clinical practice. A lot is being done in this direction, thanks to the involvement of families and the creation of international networks, such as the ERNs, which are rapidly promoting collaboration among highly specialized centers and the establishment of disease registries to shed light on the natural history of LGS. Yet, many unmet needs still afflict patients and their families, including uncertainties arising from the transition process and a lack of administrative support and comprehensive care as patients transition into adulthood. This article summarizes these key challenges in diagnosing, treating, and caring for patients with LGS, as well as the roadmap to enhanced future care discussed during the international LGS meeting held in Genoa.

**中文摘要译文：**
Lennox-Gastaut综合征（LGS）是最严重但也是讨论最多的儿童期发病的发育性和癫痫性脑病（DEEs）之一。癫痫学家对该综合征的定义和最小电临床特征集存在分歧，这源于该综合征内的高度病因异质性，可能导致其患病率被高估。然而，近年来，我们的诊断策略，包括高分辨率磁共振成像和下一代测序技术，使我们能够解析许多先前被归类为"特发性"的病例。此外，一些脑电图和循环生物标志物已被确定，如果在更大的患者群体中得到证实，可以预测疾病进展和治疗反应。随着我们诊断能力的提高，我们的治疗策略也在增加。尽管已取得进展，但在临床实践中仍需要实施更好的临床试验设计、个体化治疗以及针对疾病遗传根源的疗法。由于家庭的参与和国际网络（如ERNs）的建立，在这方面正在做很多工作，这些网络正在迅速促进高度专业化中心之间的合作，并建立疾病登记处以阐明LGS的自然史。然而，许多未满足的需求仍然困扰着患者及其家人，包括过渡过程中产生的不确定性，以及患者过渡到成年期时缺乏行政支持和全面护理。本文总结了在诊断、治疗和护理LGS患者方面的这些关键挑战，以及在热那亚举行的国际LGS会议上讨论的加强未来护理的路线图。

## 3. 不同癫痫亚型中基底节区扩大的血管周围间隙

**原文链接：** [https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41159639](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41159639)
**期刊：** Epilepsia
**PMID：** 41159639
**DOI：** 10.1111/epi.18694

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** Enlarged perivascular spaces in the basal ganglia across epilepsy subtypes.

**英文摘要原文：**
OBJECTIVE: The glymphatic system is thought to be the brain's primary waste clearance system, responsible for eliminating soluble metabolites and proteins from the central nervous system. It consists of the cerebrospinal fluid, the interstitial fluid, and a conduit between the two, perivascular spaces (PVS). PVS and glymphatics may impact the pathophysiology of epilepsy and its associated neuropsychiatric comorbidities, potentially via reduced clearance of excitotoxic substances. This study investigates enlarged PVS burden in a large patient group with various types of epilepsy.

METHODS: A total of 467 people with various types of epilepsy were recruited from the Hospital das Clínicas, University of Campinas, Brazil; 267 had temporal lobe epilepsy with hippocampal sclerosis (TLE-HS), 71 had TLE with no magnetic resonance imaging (MRI)-visible lesions, 65 had focal extratemporal epilepsy, and 64 had idiopathic generalized epilepsy. They were matched for age and sex with 473 healthy volunteers as controls. All participants were scanned with T1-weighted MRI, and a deep-learning algorithm, PINGU (Perivascular-Space Identification Nnunet for Generalized Usage), was applied to segment PVS. The volumes of PVS in the white matter (WM) and basal ganglia (BG) were calculated, and PVS volume fraction (PVS-VF) was used as a dependent variables in a general linear model, with the diagnostic group as the independent variable of interest.

RESULTS: The epilepsy group, across all subtypes, had higher PVS-VF in the BG compared to controls (101%-140%, effect size = .95-1.37, p < 3.77 × 10). There was no difference in PVS-VF in the WM between the epilepsy groups and healthy controls, or between different epilepsy subtypes. Only the TLE-HS group had a PVS-VF asymmetry in the WM, with more PVS on the contralateral side, particularly in the temporal lobes. There was no association between PVS-VF and duration of illness.

SIGNIFICANCE: There is an increase in PVS volume in the BG across broad subtypes of epilepsy, suggesting either a common mechanism of seizure generation or a common consequence of seizures.

**中文摘要译文：**
目的：类淋巴系统被认为是大脑主要的废物清除系统，负责清除中枢神经系统中的可溶性代谢物和蛋白质。它由脑脊液、组织间液以及两者之间的通道——血管周围间隙（PVS）组成。PVS和类淋巴系统可能通过减少兴奋性毒性物质的清除来影响癫痫的病理生理学及其相关的神经精神共病。本研究调查了大型癫痫患者群体中扩大的PVS负担。

方法：从巴西坎皮纳斯大学医院招募了467名各种类型癫痫患者；其中267人患有海马硬化性颞叶癫痫（TLE-HS），71人患有无磁共振成像（MRI）可见病变的TLE，65人患有局灶性颞叶外癫痫，64人患有特发性全面性癫痫。他们与473名年龄和性别匹配的健康志愿者作为对照组。所有参与者均接受T1加权MRI扫描，并应用深度学习算法PINGU（血管周围间隙识别通用神经网络）来分割PVS。计算了白质（WM）和基底节（BG）中的PVS体积，并将PVS体积分数（PVS-VF）用作一般线性模型中的因变量，以诊断组为感兴趣的自变量。

结果：与对照组相比，所有亚型的癫痫组在BG中的PVS-VF均较高（101%-140%，效应量=0.95-1.37，p<3.77×10）。癫痫组与健康对照组之间，或不同癫痫亚型之间，在WM中的PVS-VF没有差异。只有TLE-HS组在WM中存在PVS-VF不对称性，对侧（特别是颞叶）有更多PVS。PVS-VF与病程之间没有关联。

意义：在广泛的癫痫亚型中，BG中的PVS体积增加，表明癫痫发作可能具有共同的产生机制或共同的后果。

### 第二部分 AI 大师评价

本研究创新性地探讨了类淋巴系统在癫痫病理生理中的作用，通过大样本多中心研究揭示了基底节区血管周围间隙扩大是不同癫痫亚型的共同特征。研究采用深度学习算法PINGU进行精准的PVS分割，方法学先进可靠。关键发现表明基底节区PVS体积显著增加（101%-140%），而白质区无明显变化，这为理解癫痫的神经环路机制提供了新的视角。该研究具有重要的临床价值，可能为癫痫的生物标志物开发和靶向治疗提供新思路，但需注意其未能建立PVS变化与病程的关联，未来需要纵向研究来阐明其因果机制。
