## 2. 基于影像的新型风险评分（IsCHEMiA）用于预测卒中后癫痫的开发与国际验证

**原文链接：** [点击跳转](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41490217)
**期刊：** Neurology
**PMID：** 41490217
**DOI：** 10.1212/WNL.0000000000214486

### 第一部分 原文与翻译

**英文原标题：** Development and International Validation of a Novel Imaging-Based Risk Score (IsCHEMiA) for the Prediction of Poststroke Epilepsy.

> **英文摘要：**
> BACKGROUND AND OBJECTIVES: Stroke is one of the most common causes of adult-onset epilepsy. We aimed to develop a model to predict poststroke epilepsy (PSE) after a first-ever ischemic stroke, incorporating neuroimaging features of incident stroke.
> 
> METHODS: We analyzed clinical and neuroimaging features of patients with first-ever acute ischemic stroke consecutively admitted to Massachusetts General Hospital, United States. We performed competing risk regression with all-cause mortality as a competing event and derived the final multivariable model using backward stepwise elimination by the Akaike Information Criterion. We externally validated the model in 3 international cohorts in Hong Kong (Queen Mary Hospital [HK-QMH], Ruttonjee Hospital [HK-RH]) and Japan (National Cerebral and Cardiovascular Center) by discrimination and calibration and compared its performance with the SeLECT and SeLECT2.0 scores.
> 
> RESULTS: We included a final derivative cohort of 1,436 patients with a mean age of 67.4 years and a slight male predominance (54.7%), along with a total of 2,534 patients in the validation cohorts. PSE, defined as the occurrence or recurrence of unprovoked seizure >7 days after stroke, occurred in 5.5% of the overall study population. Six variables (infarct size [Is], cortical involvement [C], hemorrhagic transformation [H], early seizures [E], MCA involvement [Mi], and age younger than 65 [A]) were independent predictors included in the final model and formed the IsCHEMiA score. Model discrimination was consistent across all cohorts, with -statistics of 0.870 (United States), 0.852 (HK-QMH), 0.857 (HK-RH), and 0.826 (Japan). The model was well calibrated at 1 and 3 years after stroke in the overall validation cohort. The IsCHEMiA score improved the prediction of PSE compared with SeLECT in all cohorts and the overall study population (-statistic 0.848 vs 0.782,  = 5.170,  < 0.0001). For example, an IsCHEMiA score of 3 predicts a low risk of PSE at 1 year (2%) and 5 years (6%) while an IsCHEMiA score ≥8 predicts a high risk at 1 year (67%) and 5 years (78%).
> 
> DISCUSSION: The IsCHEMiA score is an improved and readily applicable predictive model developed and validated using international stroke cohorts in the modern era of reperfusion therapies. It serves as a foundation for personalized management and may guide future clinical trials on antiepileptogenic therapies in acute ischemic stroke.

> **中文摘要：**
> 背景与目的：卒中是成人发作性癫痫最常见的病因之一。本研究旨在开发一种模型，用于整合首次缺血性卒中的神经影像学特征，预测卒中后癫痫（PSE）的发生。
> 
> 方法：我们分析了连续收入美国麻省总医院的首次急性缺血性卒中患者的临床与神经影像学特征。以全因死亡为竞争事件，进行了竞争风险回归，并采用赤池信息准则的反向逐步消除法建立最终多变量模型。我们在香港（玛丽医院 [HK-QMH]、律敦治医院 [HK-RH]）和日本（国立脑血管中心）三组国际独立队列中，通过区分度与校准度进行外部验证，并将模型性能与 SeLECT 及 SeLECT2.0 评分进行比较。
> 
> 结果：最终推导队列共纳入1,436名患者，平均年龄为67.4岁，男性比例略高（54.7%），验证队列共计2,534名患者。PSE定义为卒中后超过7天出现或复发的非诱发性癫痫发作，发生率为总体研究人群的5.5%。最终模型包含6个独立预测变量（梗死体积[Is]、皮质受累[C]、出血性转化[H]、早期癫痫发作[E]、大脑中动脉受累[Mi]及年龄＜65岁[A]），共同组成 IsCHEMiA 评分。该模型在各队列中区分度一致：美国0.870、HK-QMH 0.852、HK-RH 0.857、日本0.826。模型在卒中后1年及3年的整体验证队列中表现出良好校准度。与 SeLECT 相比，IsCHEMiA 评分在所有队列及总体研究人群中显著提高了PSE预测能力（统计量0.848 vs 0.782，=5.170，<0.0001）。例如，IsCHEMiA 评分为3时，预测卒中后1年PSE风险为2%、5年为6%；而评分≥8时，预测1年风险为67%、5年为78%。
> 
> 讨论：IsCHEMiA 评分是一种改进且易于应用的预测模型，基于国际卒中队列在现代再灌注治疗时代开发与验证。它为个体化管理奠定基础，并可能指导急性缺血性卒中中抗癫痫发生治疗的未来临床试验。

### 第二部分 AI 大师评价

该研究基于多个国际队列开发并验证了一个新的卒中后癫痫预测模型——IsCHEMiA评分，充分利用了神经影像学变量与临床特征。研究方法严谨，采用竞争风险回归和多中心外部验证，显示该模型在不同地区均具有良好的区分度与校准性。结果表明，IsCHEMiA优于现有SeLECT评分，能为卒中后癫痫风险分层和个体化管理提供新工具。其创新性在于整合影像特征，而局限性可能在于仍需更多种族与治疗环境的验证。
